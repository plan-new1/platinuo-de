Renewables — Home Adoption Notes

Focus on efficiency first, then explore generation with qualified providers.

Solar Basics

Household solar commonly includes panels, an inverter, and optional storage.

  • Check roof orientation
  • Compare quotes carefully
  • EEG (Renewable Energy Sources Act): Check current feed-in tariffs (Einspeisevergütung) for excess power fed back into the grid.

Balcony Solar (Balkonkraftwerk)

Tenants and apartment dwellers can generate their own electricity using plug-in solar devices.

  • Registration: Simplified registration in the Marktstammdatenregister is required.
  • Plug & Play: Connects directly to a standard Schuko socket.
  • Savings: Can cover base load (fridge, router) during the day.

Wind & Other Options

Small-scale wind depends on local conditions; other technologies may suit specific contexts.

  • Review site constraints
  • Assess maintenance needs

Efficiency First

Before investing, reduce demand through insulation, sealing, and efficient devices.

  • Energy audits help
  • Prioritize long-term value

Safety & Maintenance

Qualified installers, regular checks, and warranty awareness support performance.

  • Keep records
  • Follow manufacturer guidance

FAQs

Is solar always viable?

Suitability varies by location, roof design, and budget.

Do I need batteries?

Storage is optional; weigh costs, benefits, and usage patterns.

What should I compare in quotes?

Components, warranties, installation standards, and after-sales support.
